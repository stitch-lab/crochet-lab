Understanding Our Fibers

Organic Cotton

Grown without pesticides and processed chemical-free. Cotton's breathable structure makes it perfect for summer garments, baby items, and lightweight accessories that get softer with every wash.

Baby Alpaca

Hypoallergenic, lanolin-free, and incredibly soft. Alpaca fiber has a natural silk-like sheen and creates luxurious drape for special occasion pieces that are warmer than wool yet lighter in weight.

Bamboo Silk

Naturally antibacterial with UV protection. Bamboo's smooth round fibers create a cool-to-touch fabric with beautiful fluid drape, ideal for shawls, wraps, and warm-weather wearables.

The Cardigan

Each cardigan begins as a single chain and grows into a garment that carries the weight of hundreds of hours. I use oversized silhouettes and textured stitch combinations to create pieces that feel like a hug you can wear.

Natural fibers are chosen specifically for how they age — merino softens with every wash, cotton develops a lived-in drape, and alpaca gains a luminous halo over time. These are heirlooms in the making.

Botanical Series

Botanical Lilies

Lilies represent purity and devotion. Crafted using fine cotton thread and steel hooks to achieve the delicate veining visible on each petal surface.

The stamens are hand-formed from embroidery floss and secured with tiny French knots at the center. Each lily takes approximately four hours to complete.

Botanical Series

Crocheted Tulips

Symbolizing perfect love and renewal. Each petal is shaped individually using layered single crochet stitches, then assembled around a wired stem so every bloom can be posed naturally.

The gradient color transitions mimic real tulip varieties — from deep crimson bases fading to golden tips. No two flowers are identical, just like their living counterparts. Each takes 3-4 hours to complete.

Botanical Series

The Forget Me Not

Named for the tiny blue flowers that symbolize enduring memory and true love. These miniature blooms are crocheted with lace-weight cotton thread and a 1.5mm steel hook, requiring a steadiness of hand that only comes with years of practice.

Each flower head contains over 200 individual stitches, each one placed to replicate the delicate veining of a real forget-me-not petal. They are assembled into clusters that can be worn as hair accessories, pinned to lapels, or arranged in small vases as permanent botanical art.

Crochet Pillows

Textured cushions that bring warmth and handmade character to any living space. Each pillow is a study in tactile comfort.

Texture You Can Feel

Every pillow is constructed using dense, plush stitches that create a satisfyingly soft surface. The interlocking loops form a cushioned barrier that's as comfortable to lean against as it is beautiful to look at.

Filled with hypoallergenic poly-fill and encased in removable covers, these pillows are designed for everyday living — not just display. Machine-washable covers mean they stay fresh through seasons of use.

Heritage

A Craft Through Time

1800s

European Origins

Crochet emerges as a distinct craft in France and Ireland. Irish lace crochet becomes a vital industry during the Potato Famine.

1846

First Pattern Publications

Mlle. Riego de la Branchardière publishes the first crochet pattern books, transforming oral tradition into written instruction.

1920s

The Golden Age

Elaborate collars, cloche hats, and decorative home textiles become essential fashion staples worldwide.

1970s

Bohemian Revival

Granny squares and colorful afghans define the counterculture aesthetic. Crochet becomes a symbol of handmade resistance.

2020s

Modern Renaissance

Sustainable fashion movements bring crochet into contemporary culture as both art and activism.
